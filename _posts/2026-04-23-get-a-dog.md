I'm sure that this will seem like no-brainer advice to anyone who has a dog but, if you don't have one, you should get a dog. They're the best. I don't mean that hyperbolically. My dog, Isaac, is the best thing that ever happened to me. I view my life as having two phases, before-Isaac and after-Isaac. He makes me incredibly happy, and he also makes the people around me happier. He makes my life bearable when it's not, and he makes it better when it is. Thinking about who I would be right now without him is legitimately scary. And, yes, I have a plan for when he gets old and dies (get another dog as soon as he shows signs of wear because if he dies and I'm alone when it happens, I WILL neck myself).

## What a dog is not

![dog in a jumper]({{ site.baseurl }}/images/isaac/IMG_6908.jpeg)

- A baby: Obviously. A dog is a dog, and a baby is a baby. Most importantly, it's not a human being. It's it's own thing. You don't walk a baby (or do you? I don't know, I've never owned a baby) and you're never going to see your dog win the basketball championship (shh, it doesn't matter that it's not in the rulebook). Of course there are similarities, but the point I'm making is that the benefits a dog provides you aren't the same as those a baby does. If you want a baby, go on, shoot your shot toigah. If you want a dog, it's a different shot.
- Easy: They're not hard (barring emergencies) but they put limits on certain aspects of your life. Going out, travelling, even being lazy is less fancy free than without a dog (which is a good thing, but I'll get to that). But that's just the reality of taking on a responsibility. It's a trade-off, but it's usually a good one.
- Third thing: 'Nuff said. Shut up. I want to get to the good bits.

## What a dog is 

![dog in da grass]({{ site.baseurl }}/images/isaac/IMG_5537.jpeg)

- A KICKASS sidekick (people normally refer to this as "a companion" but that's lame): I finally understand why superheroes have sidekicks. They elevate you. They push you closer to what you can be. They allow you to get back what you give. All you have to do is feed them, find them a place to shit and piss, and take them outside (oh, the humanity), and then you get them looking at you with those widdle puppy dog eyes. You get to feel their head resting on you as you (yes, you) write a shitty blog post. You get, I think, real love. The kind that those smug fucks with the "a smile is a universal language" bumper stickers could only dream of (unless they also have a dog, but I don't think they do because if they didn't then we would need their smug fuck bumper stickers).
- Stupid: This sort of follows on from my last point. I think we need more stupidity in our lives, though anyone who knows me would probably beg to differ. You can't overintellectuallise with a dog. You can share your half baked ideas with it, and the best you can hope for is a look that says, "Back to the drawing board, bucko!". They will not, and cannot, let you be anything more than a human being.
- A reminder to engage with life: I love sleeping in, but I can't because I have to get up in the morning to feed Isaac. I love becoming a human potato, but I can't because Isaac needs to be walked. The things that a dog needs from you are all, entirely, good. And, as I said, they don't need much from you, but they do need you to remember that there is a there here. They don't want much from you, but (unlike the corporations that rule our lives) commenting on obviously rage baiting content while you've got your dick in one hand with the other furiously typing "something, something KAREN" at 3 in the morning is not one of them.

## What should you do?

![dog in a jacket]({{ site.baseurl }}/images/isaac/IMG_1860.jpeg)

Get a dog.
